“all phenomena in their own-being are empty”




-Heart Sutra





The Buddhist phrase “all phenomena in their own-being are empty” is commonly misunderstood to read "all phenomena are empty".


In this sense, emptiness is the Buddhist version of nothingness.


Buddhism doesn't deny existence, and Buddhism doesn't affirm non-existence -


it is a denial of intrinsic existence.


we are all a connection, a continuation, and a whole essence


emptiness represents that nothing is different, but everything is changing at the same time




"If I am influenced, there is no such thing as a constant, fixed I,"

- Nāgārjuna







"avoid the misapprehension that emptiness is an absolute reality or an independent truth"

-Dalai Lama





When Buddhists refer to emptiness, they refer not to the Western conception of emptiness: a negative, alone feeling, but rather a connected mass – a feeling of wholeness.





One of the staples of Buddhism is the idea that nothing is fixed or permanent.




Anitya
